Ben Searle - CV

Why did you decide to become a web developer?

I went through my school and university days with no strong notion of what I wanted to do. I just thought I’d figure it out as I went along and my choice would become clear after I graduated. After a four year undergraduate degree and a 1 year postgraduate course I still had no idea what to do. I took a job in an Ecommerce department for an online retailer and quickly learned that the job wasn’t for me. I wasn’t really interested in many aspects of my role with the exception of making changes to the frontend of the website using HTML. I did some soul searching and decided that creating and improving websites for a living would be a very different and exciting career, so I left my job, found part time work and now, signed up for a course and now, here I am!

What type of projects are you most interested in?

I am passionate about sport and I have noticed in the past that finding information online about sports clubs, national governing bodies and event information is often difficult as there a lot of organisations that overlap and websites aren't always the primary focus as they are not sales based websites. I also believe there is a gap in the market for apps related to specific sports. If I could pick anything to work on, I would love to help the world of club sport improve its presence and functionality online.

What is your design process?

So far I have found that it is easy to overthink and attempt to tackle a variety of challenges at the same time. My process now is to make an overall plan for a project and to break it down into small chunks. First I will gather the content, then I will determine the layout. After I am happy with the design and the functionality, I will move on to the next section and repeat the process.

What is your average project turnaround?

I have just finished my first project - building this website - It has taken me approximately 100 hours to make it, but seeing as this is the first project, I hope to streamline my process and improve on my turn around.
